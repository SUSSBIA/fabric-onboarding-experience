# Sandbox Readiness Checklist

Use this checklist before starting any Fabric sandbox exercise.

This checklist helps ensure that learners are using the correct workspace, safe data, and appropriate artefacts before creating or modifying anything in Microsoft Fabric.

## 1. Workspace Check

- [ ] I am working in the assigned sandbox workspace
- [ ] I understand that this workspace is for learning and experimentation
- [ ] I am not working in a department workspace unless explicitly instructed
- [ ] I am not working in a BIA production workspace
- [ ] I know who to contact if I am unsure about the workspace

## 2. Data Safety Check

- [ ] I am using public, mocked, synthetic, or approved non-sensitive data
- [ ] I am not uploading confidential institutional data
- [ ] I am not uploading restricted institutional data
- [ ] I am not uploading student personal data
- [ ] I am not uploading staff personal data
- [ ] I am not uploading financial, donor, or operational production data
- [ ] I understand the sensitivity label, if one is applied

## 3. Exercise Purpose Check

- [ ] I understand the purpose of the sandbox exercise
- [ ] I know which persona pathway this exercise supports
- [ ] I know which artefact I should use
- [ ] I understand whether I am consuming, building, analysing, engineering, modelling, or reviewing
- [ ] I know what output I am expected to produce

## 4. Artefact Check

- [ ] I know whether I should use a report, PBIX, dataset, Lakehouse, notebook, template, or checklist
- [ ] I will not overwrite shared starter artefacts
- [ ] I will work on a copy where instructed
- [ ] I will name my work clearly
- [ ] I will avoid vague names such as `test`, `final`, `latest`, or `copy`

## 5. Capacity and Resource Check

- [ ] I understand that Fabric activities consume shared capacity
- [ ] I will avoid unnecessary repeated refreshes
- [ ] I will avoid running heavy workloads unless instructed
- [ ] I will stop or clean up unnecessary experiments where appropriate
- [ ] I will inform the workspace owner or BIA if I plan to run a heavy or recurring workload

## 6. Sharing and Export Check

- [ ] I understand that sandbox outputs are not official
- [ ] I will not share sandbox outputs as production-ready findings
- [ ] I will not export data unless permitted
- [ ] I will not forward screenshots without context
- [ ] I will clearly label any shared sandbox output as learning or experimental

## 7. Documentation Check

- [ ] I will document assumptions, caveats, and limitations
- [ ] I will record what data or artefact I used
- [ ] I will record what I changed or created
- [ ] I will note any issue, confusion, or question
- [ ] I will use the relevant template where appropriate

## 8. Completion Check

- [ ] I completed the exercise in sandbox
- [ ] I used only safe data
- [ ] I named my work clearly
- [ ] I documented my output
- [ ] I understand that the output is not production-ready
- [ ] I know the next pathway or experiment to continue with
